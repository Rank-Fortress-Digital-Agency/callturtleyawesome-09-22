Why you should get your heating system checked before winter


When the cold weather starts to set in, it’s important to make sure that all of your home’s systems are up for the challenge. One system that often gets overlooked is the heating system. But neglecting your heating system can lead to several problems down the line.

That’s why it’s important to get your heating system checked before winter sets in. A professional inspection can help you identify any potential problems and take care of them before they become bigger issues.

Save Money on Your Energy Bills

If your heating system isn’t running efficiently, it’s going to cost you more money to heat your home. Getting an inspection and tune-up can help improve your system’s efficiency and lower your energy bills.

If you have a forced-air heating system, there are a few things that you can do to keep it running efficiently. First, make sure that your air filters are clean. Dirty filters can restrict airflow and cause your system to work harder than it needs to. Second, have your ductwork inspected and cleaned if necessary. Leaky or damaged ducts can also cause your system to work harder than it needs to. Finally, make sure that your thermostat is set correctly. A properly-set thermostat can save you money on your energy bills.

If you have a boiler-based heating system, there are a few things that you can do to keep it running efficiently. First, make sure that your boiler is serviced regularly. A well-maintained boiler will run more efficiently than one that isn’t serviced regularly. Second, make sure that your radiators are free of dust and dirt. Dust and dirt can restrict airflow and cause your system to work harder than it needs to. Finally, make sure that your thermostat is set correctly. A properly-set thermostat can save you money on your energy bills.

Save Time and Stress


Dealing with a broken heating system in the middle of winter is never fun. But if you get your system checked before winter, you can avoid the hassle and stress of dealing with a problem in the middle of a cold snap. Here are a few tips to help you keep your heating system in good working order.

  • Check the filters monthly and replace them as needed. This is one of the simplest and most effective ways to keep your system running smoothly.
  • Have your system checked by a professional at least once a year. This will help ensure that everything is in good working order and that there are no potential problems.
  • If you have a gas heating system, make sure the pilot light is lit. This will help prevent the system from going out unexpectedly.
  • Make sure all the vents in your home are unobstructed. This includes furniture, curtains, and other items that might block the flow of air.
  • Keep your thermostat at a consistent temperature. This will help prevent your system from working harder than it needs to and will extend its life.
  • Check the area around your outdoor unit for debris. Leaves, twigs, and other items can build up and prevent the unit from working properly.
  • If you have an electric heating system, check the cords and connections regularly. Loose or damaged cords can pose a fire hazard.
  • If you have a forced air heating system, make sure the ductwork is in good condition. Leaks in the ductwork can reduce the efficiency of your system and cause your energy bills to go up.
  • If you have a boiler, check the water level regularly. This will help prevent the system from running dry and damaging the unit.
  • Always follow the manufacturer’s instructions when operating your heating system. This will help prevent accidents and ensure that your system is used properly.
